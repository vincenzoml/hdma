\begin{abstract}
 We define a class of stream languages, and the corresponding automata, that operate on infinite alphabets. The automata used for recognition are a generalisation of deterministic Muller automata to the case of infinite alphabets. Closure under complement, union and intersection, and decidability of emptyness and equivalence are preserved by the generalisation. This is shown by using finite representations of the (otherwise infinite-state) defined class of automata.
\end{abstract}
